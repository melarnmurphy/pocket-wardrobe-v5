# Pocket Wardrobe Style Guide

## Direction

Pocket Wardrobe should feel like a fashion operating system, not a beige utility app.
The visual language is:

- Gen Z bold
- premium but not sterile
- editorial rather than SaaS
- structured enough to support dashboards, rules, and data-heavy flows

## Core palette

- Deep violet: `#2D1B69`
- Electric purple: `#7B5CF0`
- Hot pink: `#FF6B9D`
- Acid yellow: `#FFD166`
- Cyber mint: `#0DFFE8`
- Ink: `#0F0D1A`
- Soft lavender: `#F5F3FF`

## Usage rules

- Deep violet and electric purple are the product foundation.
- Hot pink and acid yellow are accent energy. Use them for CTA emphasis, status moments, and hero detail.
- Cyber mint is reserved for the Trends section and trend-specific analytics states.
- The violet to purple to pink to yellow gradient is reserved for hero, banner, and premium highlight surfaces.
- Avoid reintroducing beige, amber, or earthy neutrals as primary brand colors.

## Shape system

- Buttons: pill-shaped
- Standard card radius: `8px`
- Borders: `0.5px` where possible
- Elevated surfaces should use soft violet shadows rather than brown or gray shadows

## Typography

- Display: `Space Grotesk`
- Body: `Plus Jakarta Sans`
- Use tighter tracking on headlines and spaced uppercase labels for section kickers

## Trends section

- Trends should read as a dedicated product surface, not another wardrobe tab.
- Cyber mint should appear here first and most strongly.
- Trend cards can mix mint with purple, pink, and yellow scoring states, but mint remains the anchor.

## Motion

- Prefer short editorial-feeling transitions over playful bounce.
- View transitions should use a fast eased curve.
- Interactions should feel crisp and intentional rather than overly animated.
